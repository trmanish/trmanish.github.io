<br>Going forward, I'm stepping back. But if you ever genuinely need to, you can still reach me. You don't have to, and you may not even care — that's completely fine. I just wanted to say it once.

I don't expect you'd ever need to. You're capable and know how to navigate life well. But life has its own way sometimes. <br>

**• If you do reach out, then do it normally**
<br>Like two normal people would — not just on WhatsApp. I never made an issue of it. Whatever kept us talking — the comfort, the conversations, our apparently similar taste in certain 'things' — I left it open however worked for you.

If you do reach out, talk normally. Don't let it become awkward — that goes for me too. That's how it felt when I called last week, and it shouldn't be that way if there's mutual respect. Things are simple enough as they are.

<br>

**• One thing I want to leave you with**
<br>I may have some sense of what might be going on in your personal life — though I never asked, and for the last 1.5 years I kept it as none of my business. Maybe that's also why I wanted to share that story in the earlier audio before I leave. But regardless, you already know this from me: **do not compromise your self-respect in any relationship you are in now or choose in the future**. This is the one thing I mean with complete seriousness. You can ignore everything else I've said, but not this.

There is a thin but consequential line between a dominant personality and an abusive one. The former takes charge in one aspect of a relationship — the latter needs to control all of it. Be very clear on that difference. Strength in a person isn't about controlling everything — it's about knowing where to stop.
