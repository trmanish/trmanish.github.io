## Mila,

I have a [habit of](https://trmanish.github.io/2024/06/14/things-u-learn-growing.html) transparently ending any relationship (friendship, romantic, family) out of respect for (if) any good moments spent with the other person, even if one of us walks away. Most importantly I do this so none of us ever have any lingering questions about the reasons.

In that regard, I want to state that you and I will no longer be in contact going forward. I would be lying if I said I didn't feel a bit sad about it, but I sometimes feel sad about many things in life— so this will be one more.

2 years ago when you walked away, without letting me have a moment of conversation, I sent you flowers, appreciated the moments we shared, and then moved on deleting your contact and all memories. You as a person mattered more than your leaving.

Unfortunately, I made a mistake by allowing you to contact me after a few months. When you contacted me a few months after walking away, I hesitated to continue the conversation. However, out of weakness and lingering affection and care for you, I allowed you to send me messages even those restricted to WhatsApp by you. I never questioned this, as I didn't want to bring any expectations to your actions. Maybe it was my subtle desire to know you are doing well in life.

For the last 1.5 years, our conversations have centered on you wanting to visit me, wanting to visit Yosemite together, or me visiting you in NYC. In hindsight, I didn't think much about those, as I had no expectations for anything you would write. Reason I never called you at all, nor asked you to unblock me on iMessages. You messaged, I responded. That's all I took it as. I never questioned why you reached out again. Maybe you missed the conversations, maybe you wanted company, or maybe something else. I didn't ask; I didn't want to. I kept it open as you wanted.

However, if your plan was to come to SF for someone else, there was no need to mention your trip to me — not after 1.5 years of talking about visiting me. Had you been upfront about it, I would have genuinely congratulated you. Seeing you happy and doing well was always the point.

You consciously came to SF and spent time with someone else, after giving me the impression all last year that you wanted to meet me. I cleaned my apartment in case you preferred not to stay in a hotel. Consider the reverse: I tell you I'm visiting NYC, after a year of suggesting I'd come see you, only to quietly spend time with another woman without so much as a message. You may not care who I spend my time with — after all, it's my life, my prerogative. But you might feel a little irritated. Transparency isn't that hard and most people will only cheer you on when they see you're genuinely happy.

If your intent was to cause me pain — you succeeded. I'm already carrying enough, most importantly not being able to see my mother, so I didn't need another. But such is life. I can't control your actions, but I can control mine — and I won't spend another second on you. You will never hear from me again, nor will I allow you to contact me in any form.

Trust when broken, seldom regains. Broken homes, don't build the same way, as heart that built them in the first place. And transparency is the foundation of Trust. The least a man can expect is transparency especially when he isn't expecting anything else from you. When you met me that night 2 years ago, while driving you to the airport, I said: A man should be the biggest cushion when a woman falls, the biggest cheerleader when she succeeds. When you disrespect that trust, he fails to have the strength to cheer you on.

I wish you the best and hope the guy you decided to spend time with was worth all this.

My love and regards to Alena, your Mum and your family.

**Goodbye!**
